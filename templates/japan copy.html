Japan

Land of the rising sun

Travel Information



Geography

On the Asian continent's east coast is the island nation of Japan. Japan is the tenth most populated country in the world with a population of about 127 million. Mountains cover around 70% of the continent, while population centers have grown up around the remaining 30%. Urban areas have a high urban population density since 75% of the population resides there.

Climate

Japan has four distinct seasons, each with its own unique characteristics. The first is a chilly, dry winter, with average temperatures ranging from about -3 to 3 degrees Celsius in the northern part of the country to 5 to 12 degrees Celsius in the south. This is followed by a mild spring that brings the famous cherry blossom season. The rainy season arrives in late June or early July, followed by hot, humid weather in August, with average temperatures ranging from about 20 to 30 degrees Celsius throughout the country. September brings typhoons and heavy rain, signaling the beginning of fall.

Cultural Etiquette

When visiting Japan, it is important to observe local customs and norms. For example, it is considered polite to not chat loudly on public transit, not to tip when eating or shopping, to stand on the correct side of the escalator, and to take off your shoes when visiting Japanese homes. By following these customs, you can help ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Japan.

Transportation

Japan is well known for their effecient public transportations and are many ways to get around. The best way to travel around is by train. Japan has a network or railways that spans throughout the country. Tourists can travel between major cities within the matter of minutes. Signs and announcements in the trains and train stations are also in English. It is recommended to buy the Japan Rail Pass as it allows you to travel by train as many times as you want in the duration of the pass being valid.

Daily Costs

One can expect to spend around 8,000 - 20,000 yen (50 to 120 GBP) on your daily expenses which includes meals, transport, fares for attractions and accommodation. For that budget, you would be able to eat meals in local restaurants, stay in business hotels and even visit many attractions. You can see our reccommended places to visit in the next section.

Attractions


Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden


Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden is a large park located in Tokyo, Japan. It features a variety of gardens, including a French, an English, and a traditional Japanese garden. The park also has a greenhouse and a teahouse.


Imperial Palace


The Imperial Palace is the primary residence of the Emperor of Japan. It is located in the center of Tokyo and includes a number of buildings. The palace is surrounded by moats and walls. Visitors are only allowed to view the compound from the outside.

Sky Tree


The Tokyo Skytree is a broadcasting and observation tower in Tokyo, Japan. It is the tallest structure in Japan and the second tallest in the world at 634 meters. The Skytree features observation decks at 350 and 450 meters, as well as a variety of restaurants, shops, and other attractions.

Mount Fuji


Mount Fuji is the highest mountain in Japan and an active stratovolcano. It is located near Tokyo and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is a popular tourist destination, especially for climbers and campers.

Food









Curry Rice

It is the national dish of Japan and it consists of rice topped with a thick, savory curry sauce. The sauce is made from mixed vegetables, meat, and spices.

Takoyaki

Small balls of batter that are filled with diced octopus, tempura, and green onions. Often topped with a variety of sauces, seasonings, and green laver seaweed

Taiyaki

Fish-shaped waffle with sweet filling. Most popular filling are chocolate, custard and red bean paste.

Tonkotsu Ramen

Noodles with a rich pork bone broth topped with ingredients such as sliced pork belly, green onions, seawead, and boiled eggs.

Daifuku

Mochi with filling. The most common filling for daifuku is sweetened red bean paste, but it can also be made with other ingredients such as fruit, chocolate, or ice cream.

Gyudon

A bowl of rice topped with sweet and savory braised sliced beef with caramelised onions.
