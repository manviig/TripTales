Uttarakhand

Land of Gods, Land of Wonders

Travel Information



Geography

Uttarakhand, located in the northern part of India, is known for its picturesque Himalayan landscapes, sacred temples, and diverse flora and fauna. It is home to the famous hill stations of Nainital, Mussoorie, and Rishikesh, and the revered Char Dham pilgrimage sites.

Climate

The climate of Uttarakhand varies from sub-tropical in the lower regions to alpine in the higher altitudes. Summers (March to June) are pleasant, while winters (November to February) bring snowfall in the higher regions, making it a popular winter destination.

Cultural Etiquette

Uttarakhand has a rich cultural heritage with traditions influenced by the mountainous terrain. While visiting, it's important to respect local customs, such as dressing modestly when visiting temples and interacting with the local communities.

Transportation

Uttarakhand is well-connected by road and rail, with buses and taxis being common modes of transport. The nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun, which connects the state to major cities in India.

Daily Costs

Daily expenses in Uttarakhand can vary widely. Budget travelers can find affordable accommodation and food, while mid-range travelers can expect slightly higher costs for meals and stays in well-known tourist destinations.

Attractions in Uttarakhand

Anusuya Devi Temple

Located near Chopta, Uttarakhand, the Anusuya Devi Temple is dedicated to Goddess Anusuya. Surrounded by lush greenery, it offers a peaceful ambiance and is a popular pilgrimage site for devotees.

Devalgarh Temple

The Devalgarh Temple, located near Srinagar in Uttarakhand, is famous for its ancient architecture. It is an important religious and historical site, attracting visitors interested in culture and spirituality.

Latu Devta Temple

Located in Chamoli, Uttarakhand, the Latu Devta Temple is associated with the Nanda Devi pilgrimage. The temple is deeply revered by the locals, especially during the Nanda Devi Mela.

Purnagiri Temple

Situated in Champawat, Uttarakhand, the Purnagiri Temple is revered by locals and holds great spiritual significance. The temple offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and is a popular pilgrimage site.

Food









Aloo Ke Gutke

A popular Uttarakhandi dish made with boiled potatoes, which are sautéed with spices like cumin, coriander, and turmeric to give them a tangy, flavorful taste.

Bal Mithai

A traditional sweet from Uttarakhand, Bal Mithai is made with khoya (milk solids) and coated with white sugar balls, often served as a treat during festivals and special occasions.

Phanu

Phanu is a nutritious dish made with rice, lentils, and a blend of spices. It is a staple in the Garhwali cuisine and is often paired with chutney or curd.

Kafuli

Kafuli is a healthy, green curry made with leafy vegetables like spinach and fenugreek. It's typically served with rice or chapati and is rich in vitamins and minerals.

Dubuk

Dubuk is a thick lentil-based curry from Uttarakhand, cooked with a variety of lentils, spices, and sometimes vegetables. It is often enjoyed with rice.

Baadi

Baadi is a traditional Uttarakhandi dish made from buckwheat flour. It's often served as a side dish with curries and vegetables, providing a wholesome and filling meal.
