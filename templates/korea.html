South Korea

A Land of Dynamic Cities and Tranquil Traditions

Travel Information



Geography

South Korea is located on the southern half of the Korean Peninsula in East Asia. It is bordered by North Korea to the north, surrounded by the Sea of Japan to the east and the Yellow Sea to the west. South Korea's terrain includes mountains, plains, and coastal areas, with Seoul as its bustling capital.

Climate

South Korea experiences four distinct seasons: spring, summer, autumn, and winter. Summers (June to August) are hot and humid, while winters (December to February) are cold and snowy, particularly in the northern regions. Spring and autumn offer mild temperatures and are considered the best times to visit.

Cultural Etiquette

South Korea values respect and tradition. Bowing is a common form of greeting, and addressing people by their titles or last names is customary. Removing shoes before entering homes or traditional spaces is standard. When dining, use both hands to accept or offer items as a sign of respect.

Transportation

South Korea has a modern transportation system, including high-speed trains (KTX), subways, buses, and taxis. The public transport system is efficient, with apps like KakaoMap or Naver Map providing real-time navigation. The T-Money card is a convenient payment method for public transit.

Daily Costs

Daily expenses in South Korea range from ₩50,000 to ₩150,000 (40 to 120 USD) depending on your travel style. Budget travelers can enjoy affordable street food and guesthouses, while mid-range options include hotels and restaurants. Many tourist attractions have reasonable entry fees.

Attractions

Gyeongbokgung Palace

Located in Seoul, this majestic palace is a symbol of South Korea's rich history. Visitors can experience traditional architecture and cultural performances here.

Jeju Island

Known as the "Hawaii of Korea," Jeju Island offers stunning beaches, volcanic landscapes, and picturesque waterfalls.

Namsan Tower

This iconic observation tower in Seoul provides panoramic views of the city, especially stunning at night.

Bukchon Hanok Village

Step back in time in this historic village featuring traditional Korean houses (hanok) nestled within modern Seoul.

Food









Kimchi

A staple of Korean cuisine, kimchi is a fermented vegetable dish, usually made with napa cabbage and radishes.

Bibimbap

A hearty dish featuring a mix of rice, vegetables, egg, and meat, all topped with spicy gochujang sauce.

Bulgogi

Marinated beef grilled to perfection, Bulgogi is one of the most popular Korean barbecue dishes.

Mandu

Korean dumplings filled with vegetables, meat, or seafood, often served steamed, fried, or in soups.
