Russia

Travel Information



Geography

Russia is the largest country in the world, spanning over 17 million square kilometers. It is located in both Europe and Asia, bordered by many countries, including China, Mongolia, and Ukraine. Russia has a population of over 144 million people, and its capital is Moscow. The country is known for its vast landscapes, including tundra, forests, and mountain ranges.

Climate

Russia experiences a wide range of climates, from Arctic in the north to temperate in the south. Winters are long and cold in most regions, with temperatures often dropping below freezing. The southern areas, such as Sochi, enjoy a more mild climate, with hot summers and mild winters. Travelers should prepare for cold temperatures, especially in winter.

Cultural Etiquette

Russian culture is influenced by a rich history and diverse ethnic groups. Visitors should be respectful, especially when it comes to greetings and formalities. A firm handshake is the typical greeting, and it is polite to use formal titles when addressing someone. Russians appreciate good manners and a respectful attitude toward their culture.

Transportation

Traveling in Russia can be done via train, bus, or plane. The Trans-Siberian Railway is famous for its scenic routes. Russia’s metro systems, especially in cities like Moscow, are efficient and widely used. When traveling long distances, trains or flights are recommended.

Daily Costs

The cost of traveling in Russia can vary greatly depending on the region. Major cities like Moscow and St. Petersburg tend to be more expensive, while smaller towns are more affordable. On average, you can expect to pay around 1000 to 2000 RUB for a meal at a mid-range restaurant, and accommodation can range from 2000 to 5000 RUB per night in a 3-star hotel.

Attractions


Red Square


Red Square is the main city square in Moscow, Russia. It is surrounded by important landmarks such as the Kremlin and St. Basil's Cathedral. The square has played a key role in Russia’s history and is one of the most visited tourist attractions in the country.


The Hermitage Museum


The Hermitage Museum in St. Petersburg is one of the largest and oldest museums in the world. It houses an extensive collection of art, including works by Leonardo da Vinci, Rembrandt, and Michelangelo.

Lake Baikal


Lake Baikal is the world's deepest freshwater lake, located in Siberia. It is renowned for its crystal-clear waters, unique biodiversity, and surrounding natural beauty. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Peterhof Palace


Peterhof Palace, located in St. Petersburg, is a magnificent palace and park complex built by Peter the Great. It is often referred to as the "Russian Versailles" due to its grand design and impressive fountains.

Food









Borscht

A traditional Russian beet soup, often served with sour cream and garnished with fresh herbs. It is a beloved dish across Russia and Eastern Europe.

Pelmeni

Dumplings typically filled with minced meat, such as beef, pork, or lamb, and served with sour cream or butter.

Salmon

Salmon is popular in Russian cuisine, often served smoked, grilled, or in salads, particularly in the northern regions of the country.

Schnitzel

A breaded and fried meat cutlet, typically made with pork or veal, and often served with potatoes and vegetables.

Olivier Salad

A popular Russian salad made with boiled potatoes, eggs, peas, pickles, and mayonnaise. It is often served during holidays.
